BudgetSeal Help Guide

Everything you need to know about managing your budget with BudgetSeal.

What is BudgetSeal?

BudgetSeal is an envelope budgeting app. The idea is simple: every dollar you earn gets a job. You divide your money into virtual envelopes — groceries, rent, entertainment — and spend from them. When an envelope is empty, you've reached your limit for that category.

The app works entirely offline. Your data lives on your device, not on a server. You can optionally sync to Google Drive, OneDrive, or Dropbox if you want a backup or to share with a partner.

The basic flow:
  1. Add accounts — where your money lives (bank, cash, credit card)
  2. Create envelopes — one for each spending category
  3. Fund envelopes — distribute your income into envelopes
  4. Spend — each expense automatically draws from its envelope

Setting Up

When you open BudgetSeal for the first time, a 3-step setup walks you through everything:

Step 1: Welcome

You'll see a quick overview of how envelope budgeting works. From here you can:

  • Get Started — set up a new household
  • Restore from Cloud — restore an existing backup from Google Drive or a file
  • Join a Household — enter an invite code to join someone else's BudgetSeal

Step 2: Your Household

Configure the basics:

  • Household name — a label for your budget (e.g., "My Budget" or "Family")
  • Base currency — the main currency you use (USD, EUR, etc.)
  • Period start day — when your monthly budget cycle starts (1st, 15th, etc.)
  • First account — name, type (Cash/Bank/Credit/Digital), and opening balance

Under More options (tap to expand), you can also customize:

  • Categories — start with a full set of 30 categories, or create your own from scratch
  • Entry mode — choose Assisted (step-by-step) or Classic (all fields at once)
You can change all of these later in Settings. Don't overthink it — just pick what feels right and adjust as you go.

Step 3: Done

You're ready to go! The app opens to your dashboard.

Accounts

Accounts represent where your money physically lives — a bank account, your wallet, a credit card, or a digital wallet (PayPal, Venmo, etc.).

Creating an Account

  1. Go to More tab → tap Accounts
  2. Tap the + button
  3. Enter a name (e.g., "Chase Checking")
  4. Choose a type: Cash, Bank, Credit card, or Digital wallet
  5. Set the currency and opening balance
  6. Tap Create

Account Detail

Tap any account to see its transactions and manage it. From the 3-dot menu, you can:

  • Adjust Balance — correct the balance without creating a transaction
  • Archive — hide the account (transactions are preserved)
  • Convert Back & Close — for travel wallets, return remaining money and archive

Archived Accounts

When you archive an account, it disappears from the main list but its transactions are preserved. To see or restore archived accounts:

  1. On the Accounts screen, tap the 3-dot menu (top-right)
  2. Tap Show Archived
  3. Archived accounts appear in a separate section at the bottom, dimmed
  4. Tap the unarchive icon next to an account to restore it
  5. Tap the menu again → Hide Archived to hide the section

Decimal Precision

Some currencies use 0 decimal places (JPY, KRW), others use 3 (BHD, KWD). BudgetSeal auto-detects this based on the currency. You can override it per account if needed:

  1. Open the account → 3-dot menu → Edit
  2. Change the Decimal Places dropdown (Auto, 0, 1, 2, or 3)

Transactions

Every time money moves — you spend, earn, or transfer — you record a transaction. BudgetSeal supports three types:

TypeWhat it does
ExpenseMoney leaves an account (and its linked envelope)
IncomeMoney enters an account (goes to Unallocated)
TransferMoney moves between two accounts

Two Entry Modes

Assisted mode (default) walks you through step by step. A progress indicator (dots) shows which step you're on:

  1. Step 1 — Choose expense/income/transfer, then enter a title (the app may auto-detect the category)
  2. Step 2 — Pick or confirm the category
  3. Step 3 — Enter the amount, select an account, and tap Save

Classic mode shows all fields on one screen — useful for complex entries with multiple items, notes, or foreign currencies.

To switch modes: go to More → Settings & Customization → Entry Mode.

Adding a Transaction (Classic Mode)

  1. Tap the + button on the Activity tab (opens expense form directly; long-press for income or transfer)
  2. Choose Expense, Income, or Transfer
  3. Fill in the title, date, and optional note (three separate cards at the top)
  4. Select a category — tap the category area to open the category picker
  5. Enter the amount using the calculator
  6. Select an account
  7. Tap Save

Multi-Item Transactions (Splits)

Need to split a grocery receipt across categories? Use the "Add item" button to add multiple line items, each with its own amount, category, and optionally a different account.

Transfers Between Accounts

  1. Choose Transfer as the type
  2. Select the From and To accounts
  3. Enter the amount
  4. If the accounts are in different currencies, an exchange rate field appears — enter how much the destination receives

Duplicate Detection

Before saving, BudgetSeal checks if a transaction with the same amount, category, and date already exists. If a possible duplicate is found, a dialog shows the existing transaction's details so you can decide whether to save or cancel.

Auto-fill

When you pick a category, BudgetSeal can automatically fill in the account, title, and amount from your last transaction with that category. Configure what gets auto-filled in Settings & Customization → Auto-fill.

Receipt Scanning

In Classic mode, tap the camera icon next to the title field to scan a receipt. The app uses offline OCR to extract text — no internet required. You can also attach receipt photos to any transaction.

Duplicate Detection

If you try to save a transaction with the same amount, category, and date as an existing one, BudgetSeal will warn you. You can still save it if it's intentional.

Managing Transactions

  • Edit — tap a transaction, then tap the edit icon
  • Delete — swipe left on a transaction, or use the context menu (long-press)
  • Duplicate — long-press → Duplicate
  • Bulk delete — long-press to enter selection mode, select multiple, then delete
  • Search — tap the search icon on the Activity tab
  • Filter — by type (All/Income/Expense/Transfer), date range, or amount range
Screenshot: Classic transaction form showing title, date, note cards, category, amount, and account

Envelopes (Budgeting)

Envelopes are the heart of BudgetSeal. They don't move money between accounts — they help you plan how to use the money you already have.

Think of it like putting cash into labeled envelopes: $500 for groceries, $1,200 for rent, $100 for entertainment. When you spend, the money comes out of the right envelope.

Two Types of Envelopes

TypeUse caseBehavior
SpendingGroceries, fuel, diningSet a monthly budget. Resets each period.
FlexibleAnything elseSet an optional target or leave open. Accumulates over time.
Envelopes plan how to use the money you already have — they don't move money between accounts. To save toward a specific goal (vacation, emergency fund) or track debts, use Goals & Loans instead. Goals create real transactions that move money.

Creating an Envelope

  1. Go to the Budget tab
  2. Tap the + button
  3. Enter a name and optionally pick an emoji icon
  4. Choose Spending or Flexible
  5. Set a budget amount (spending) or optional target (flexible)
  6. Pick a currency
  7. Link categories — expenses in these categories will automatically debit this envelope
  8. Tap Create Envelope

Envelope Settings

Tap an envelope, then use the 3-dot menu → Edit Settings to change:

  • Periodic vs Permanent — periodic resets each month; permanent accumulates
  • Rollover balance — carry remaining funds to the next period
  • Auto-reset — reset automatically at period start, or manually review

Envelope Detail Screen

Tap any envelope to see:

  • Balance card — how much is available, with a progress bar
  • Fund button — add money from your unallocated pool
  • Recent activity — funded, spent, adjustments, and resets
  • Spending history — a chart of past periods

The 3-dot menu also has:

  • Withdraw — move money back to Unallocated (flexible envelopes)
  • Revalue Balances — update exchange rates for foreign-currency balances
  • Archive — hide the envelope without deleting history
  • Delete — permanently remove
When an envelope is overspent, it shows a red border. When a savings envelope reaches its goal, you'll see a confetti celebration!

Categories

Categories organize your transactions. BudgetSeal uses a two-level system: parent categories (groups) and subcategories.

For example: Food & Dining (parent) → Groceries, Restaurants, Coffee & Snacks (subcategories).

Managing Categories

  1. Go to More → Categories
  2. Tap the + button to add a new category
  3. Enter a name, choose a parent (or make it top-level), pick an emoji icon and color
  4. Set it as Expense or Income type

Linking to Envelopes

Categories become powerful when linked to envelopes. When you spend on "Groceries" and that category is linked to your "Food" envelope, the expense automatically debits the envelope.

Link categories when creating or editing an envelope (see Envelopes).

Category Icons

Pick from 120+ curated emojis organized by group, or type any emoji in the text field. The app shows the emoji on transaction tiles and category lists.

Screenshot: Categories screen showing parent groups with expandable subcategories

Funding Envelopes

When you receive income, it goes to your Unallocated pool — money that hasn't been assigned to any envelope yet. The goal is to distribute it all.

The Unallocated card on the Budget tab shows your base currency amount prominently. If you have unallocated funds in other currencies, you'll see a "+ N other currencies" link — tap it (or the arrow) to expand and see the breakdown per currency.

Funding from the Dashboard

  1. Tap the Fund quick action on the Home tab
  2. You'll see all your envelopes with their current balances
  3. Enter how much to assign to each envelope
  4. Tap Fund to confirm

Funding a Single Envelope

  1. Go to the Budget tab
  2. Tap an envelope to open its detail screen
  3. Tap the Fund button on the balance card
  4. Enter the amount and tap Fund
If you try to fund more than your unallocated balance, BudgetSeal will warn you. You can still proceed — your unallocated balance will go negative, which means you've assigned more than you have.

Period Transitions

At the start of each budget period (based on your period start day), periodic envelopes can be reset. You choose what happens to leftover balances:

  • Return to Unallocated — the leftover goes back to the pool
  • Carry Forward — keep the balance for the next period
  • Move to... — transfer the leftover to another envelope (e.g., savings)

Envelopes with Auto-reset enabled handle this automatically. Others will trigger a banner on the Budget tab showing exactly how many envelopes need your review (e.g., "3 envelopes need reset").

Recurring Transactions

Set up transactions that repeat on a schedule — rent, salary, utility bills, etc.

Creating a Recurring Transaction

  1. Go to More → Recurring
  2. Tap the + button
  3. Fill in the details: type, title, amount, account, category
  4. Set the frequency (daily, weekly, monthly, yearly) and interval
  5. Pick a start date
  6. Tap Save

Recurring transactions are auto-posted when they're due. You can enable or disable them with the toggle switch.

Bill Calendar

Go to More → Bill Calendar to see a monthly calendar view of all your upcoming bills. Tap a day to see what's due.

Upcoming Bills

Go to More → Upcoming Bills to see all bills sorted by due date with urgency indicators:

  • Red — Overdue
  • Amber — Due today or tomorrow
  • Green — Due in a few days
Screenshot: Upcoming Bills screen showing bills with colored urgency badges

Subscriptions

Subscriptions are recurring transactions with extra tracking — price history, annual cost, and cancellation dates.

Creating a Subscription

  1. Go to More → Subscriptions
  2. Tap the + button — a "New Subscription" form opens directly
  3. Enter the service name, amount, frequency (weekly/monthly/yearly), and account
  4. Optionally set a start date and end date
  5. Tap Create

Subscription Detail

Tap a subscription to see:

  • Annual cost — calculated from the frequency and amount
  • Price history — a timeline of price changes (logged automatically when you edit the amount)
  • Past transactions — all payments made
  • Upcoming — next scheduled payment

You can Pause (disable) or set a Cancellation Date from the detail screen.

Planned Payments

Plan future one-time payments without affecting your current balances. Use this to prepare for next month's known expenses — rent, insurance, large purchases — so you can see what's coming before committing.

Creating a Planned Payment

  1. Go to More → Planned Payments
  2. Tap the + button
  3. Select the type (Expense, Income, or Transfer)
  4. Enter the amount, account, and category
  5. Choose the target month — defaults to next month
  6. Optionally pick an exact date within that month
  7. Tap Plan Payment

How Planned Payments Work

  • No balance impact — planned payments don't affect account balances, envelope balances, or reports until posted
  • Envelope preview — the Budget tab shows "$X planned" on envelopes that have planned expenses linked to their categories
  • Transaction list — planned payments appear dimmed at the top of the transaction list with a "Planned" badge, separate from real transactions

Posting (Confirming) a Payment

When you're ready to make the payment real:

  • Single — swipe right on a planned payment, or tap it and select "Post"
  • Bulk — tap "Post All" on a month header to post all planned payments for that month at once

Posting creates a real transaction through the budgeting engine — envelope balances update, account balances change, and it appears in reports.

Editing & Deleting

  • Edit — tap a planned payment to modify the amount, category, date, or other details
  • Delete — swipe left to remove a planned payment you no longer need

Templates

Templates save frequent transactions for one-tap reuse. Instead of filling in "Morning coffee, $4.50, Cash, Coffee & Snacks" every day, save it as a template.

Creating a Template

  1. Go to More → Templates
  2. Tap the + button
  3. Enter the transaction details: title, type, amount, account, category
  4. Tap Save Template

Using a Template

  • From the Templates screen — tap a template → Use template
  • From the Dashboard — your most-used templates appear in the Quick Templates section
  • When adding a transaction — tap the template icon in the form

Templates can be sorted by most used, A-Z, newest, or highest amount. You can also group them by type or category.

Goals & Loans

Track savings goals and money you've lent or borrowed — separate from your envelope budgeting.

Goals

A goal is a savings target: emergency fund, vacation, new laptop, etc.

  1. Go to More → Goals & Loans (also accessible from the Budget tab if you have existing goals)
  2. Tap Add → select Goal
  3. Enter a name, target amount, currency, and optional deadline
  4. Tap Save

To fund a goal:

  1. Open the goal — you'll see a progress card with the current amount and a progress bar
  2. Tap Add Funds
  3. Choose the account to save from
  4. Optionally pick a category for the transaction
  5. Enter the amount and confirm
  6. A real transaction is created — it appears in your transaction history

The detail screen shows a summary card (currency, deadline, remaining) and a payment history listing all recorded transactions. Payments are linked to the goal by an internal ID, so renaming the goal won't break the history. To edit the goal's settings, tap the 3-dot menu → Edit Settings.

Loans

Track money you've lent to someone or borrowed from someone.

  1. Tap Add → select Loan
  2. Enter the loan name, person, amount, and direction (I lent or I borrowed) — a hint below explains what each direction means
  3. Tap Save

To record payments:

  1. Open the loan
  2. Tap Record Payment
  3. Select the account, optionally a category, and enter the amount
  4. The payment appears in the payment history below
The category you pick for a payment is remembered for next time on the same objective. This keeps your payments consistently categorized.
Screenshot: Loan detail showing progress card, summary info, and payment history list

Travel Exchange

Going on a trip and need to exchange currency? BudgetSeal creates a temporary travel wallet to track your spending abroad.

Exchanging Money

  1. Go to More → Travel Exchange
  2. Select the account you're exchanging from
  3. Enter how much you're exchanging
  4. Choose the travel currency (the currency of your destination)
  5. Enter how much you received in the foreign currency
  6. Tap Exchange & Create Travel Wallet

A travel wallet account is created automatically. Use it to track your spending during the trip.

Coming Back

When you return and have leftover money:

  1. Go to Accounts → open the travel wallet
  2. Tap the 3-dot menu → Convert Back & Close
  3. Enter how much you got back in your base currency
  4. The travel wallet is archived
If you travel to the same country again, BudgetSeal will offer to reactivate the existing travel wallet instead of creating a new one.

Bill Splitter

Split a restaurant bill, shared groceries, or any group expense.

Manual Split

  1. Go to More → Bill Splitter (or tap Split on the dashboard)
  2. Add people — type their names
  3. Add items — enter what was ordered and the price
  4. Assign each item to the people who shared it
  5. Optionally add a tip (percentage or fixed amount)
  6. See each person's total — tap Create Transaction to log your share

Receipt Scanning

  1. Tap Scan Receipt
  2. Take a photo or pick from gallery
  3. The app extracts line items using offline OCR — no internet needed
  4. Review the detected items, fix any misreads, and assign to people
The bill splitter supports cross-currency splits. If the bill is in a foreign currency, enter the exchange rate and your share will be converted to your base currency.

Age of Money

Age of Money tells you how many days your money sits in your accounts before you spend it. It traces each expense back to the income that funded it (oldest income first).

What the numbers mean:
  • Under 14 days — you're spending almost as fast as you earn
  • 14–30 days — you have a small cushion, getting ahead
  • 30–60 days — you're spending last month's income — great!
  • 60+ days — strong financial health, big safety net

The goal is to increase this number over time.

Your Age of Money appears on the dashboard status card and in the Reports → Insights tab.

Health Check

A diagnostic tool that verifies your data is consistent.

  1. Go to More → Settings & Customization → Health Check
  2. The app checks: balance invariants, orphan entries, backup status
  3. If issues are found, you can tap Repair Balances to fix discrepancies
  4. Purge Deleted permanently removes soft-deleted transactions
  5. Use the export button to share a diagnostic report
Run Health Check if something looks off — like a balance that doesn't match what you expect. It's safe to run anytime.

Cloud Sync

Sync your data to the cloud so you have a backup or can share with a partner. BudgetSeal uses a single JSON file — no server, no account registration.

Setting Up Sync

  1. Go to More → Settings & Customization → Cloud Sync
  2. Choose a provider:
    • Google Drive — sign in with your Google account
    • OneDrive — requires the OneDrive app installed
    • Dropbox — requires the Dropbox app installed
    • Local File — pick any file on your device
  3. Tap to connect — the app creates a sync file automatically

Once connected, the app syncs automatically when you open or close it. You can also tap Sync Now anytime.

Sync Encryption

For extra security, encrypt your sync file with a password:

  1. On the Cloud Sync screen, find the Sync Encryption card
  2. Tap Enable
  3. Enter a strong password and confirm it
  4. The next sync will encrypt the file with AES-256
If you share a household with someone, both devices must use the same sync password.

Sharing a Household

Share your BudgetSeal data with a partner:

  1. On the Cloud Sync screen, tap Share Household
  2. Enter their email (for Google Drive sharing) or generate an invite code
  3. They enter the code during onboarding → Join a Household

Backup & Restore

Automatic Backups

  1. Go to More → Settings → Backup & Restore
  2. Enable Automatic Backups
  3. Set the frequency (every 6 hours to weekly)
  4. Set retention (how many backups to keep, 3 to 30)

Backups are saved locally on your device and run automatically when you open or close the app (if the frequency interval has passed). You can see all backups at the bottom of the screen and restore or delete any of them.

Manual Backup

Tap Export & Share to create a backup file and share it via any app (email, messaging, etc.).

Restoring

  • From a local backup — tap the restore icon next to any backup in the list
  • From a file — tap Restore from File and pick a .db file
Always make a backup before restoring — the restore replaces ALL current data and cannot be undone.

Web Companion

Turn your phone into a local server and manage your budget from a laptop browser. Useful for fast data entry with a real keyboard.

How It Works

Your phone serves a web page over your local WiFi network. No internet needed — both devices just need to be on the same WiFi.

Starting the Server

  1. Go to More → Web Companion
  2. Set a 4-digit PIN (required for security)
  3. Tap Start Server
  4. A URL appears (e.g., http://192.168.1.5:7432)
  5. Open that URL on your laptop browser, or scan the QR code
  6. Enter the PIN on the web page

What You Can Do on the Web

  • View and add transactions (with full form: type, account, category, amount, date, note)
  • Manage categories and accounts
  • View and fund envelopes
  • Manage recurring transactions and subscriptions
  • View reports with charts
  • Export transactions as CSV
  • Switch between light and dark mode

Security

  • PIN is required — 5 failed attempts lock the interface for 30 minutes
  • Only devices on the same WiFi network can connect
  • Server stops automatically after 6 hours
  • On Android, the server runs in the background (you can lock your phone)
  • On iOS, keep the app in the foreground — iOS doesn't allow background servers
Avoid using Web Companion on public WiFi (airports, hotels, cafes). The traffic is not encrypted, so someone on the same network could potentially see your data.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between accounts and envelopes?

Accounts are where your money physically is (bank, wallet, credit card). Envelopes are how you plan to use it. You might have $5,000 across two bank accounts, but envelopes decide: $2,000 for rent, $500 for groceries, $300 for entertainment, and $2,200 ready to assign.

What does "Unallocated" mean?

It's money in your accounts that hasn't been assigned to any envelope yet. When you receive income, it goes to Unallocated. Your goal is to distribute it to envelopes so every dollar has a purpose.

Can I use multiple currencies?

Yes. Each account has its own currency. When you transfer between accounts in different currencies, an exchange rate field appears. Exchange rates are fetched automatically and cached for one hour. Reports and summaries show totals per currency — they're never mixed.

Is my data sent anywhere?

No. Everything stays on your device. Cloud sync is optional and uses your own Google Drive, OneDrive, or Dropbox — BudgetSeal never sees your data. There are no servers, no accounts, and no tracking.

What happens if I delete a transaction?

Deleted transactions are soft-deleted — they're hidden but not permanently removed. Any envelope deductions are reversed. You can permanently purge them from Health Check.

Can two people share a budget?

Yes. Set up Cloud Sync with Google Drive on one device, then share the household with your partner. Both devices sync to the same file. Changes merge automatically — the newer edit wins.

What if I overspend an envelope?

The envelope goes negative and shows a red indicator. Your transaction still saves — BudgetSeal never blocks spending. It's a signal to either fund the envelope more or spend less.

How do I change my base currency?

Go to More → Settings & Customization → Base Currency. Note: this changes the display currency for summaries and reports. Existing transactions keep their original currency.

My notifications aren't working

Make sure notifications are enabled in your device settings for BudgetSeal. On Android 13+, you need to grant notification permission explicitly. Go to Settings → Notifications to configure daily reminders and bill alerts.
